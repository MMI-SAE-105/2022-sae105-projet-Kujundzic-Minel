When a pop/rnb star pays tribute to a battered country.

Screams of Bosnia, was written in the midst of the tragic events in Ex-Yugoslavia by the group Le silence des mosquées, in 2007. This group composed of Karim, Aziz, Kader and Khalid sang with the aim of entertaining themselves and with their passion for singing. However, in 1996, the group realized that their songs had a real impact, so they decided to continue recording music to expand their impact and to shed light on tragedies such as tragedies such as the war and genocide in Bosnia and Herzegovina.

Born on July 8, 1986 in Bejaïa, in Kabylia, Farah of her real first name, is originally from the northern districts of Marseille. It is on the tray of Skyrock that the star of the time met for the first time the group of Muslim artists. Cris de Bosnie is a music sung in choir by its authors. The version in feat with Kenza Farah is sung by her and the chorus with the group. The singer to bring to the music not only more visibility but also a personal touch with her melancholic voice and a more dynamic tempo. Indeed, being in the RnB style, the production production resembles more the young woman and her other works. The lyrics are even more touching with the feminine and poignant voice of Kenza Farah. Indeed, Cries of Bosnia is a choir that testifies to the torture and massacre that has undergone during the war of 1992-1995, but also during the genocide of Srebrenica in July 1995.
